The McIntosh Test



The following questions are adapted from the incredible work of activist Peggy McIntosh, and her writing analyzing white privilege in "Unpacking the Invisible Knapsack", found online here





Answer the following True or False questions honestly and critically. It may require some uncomfortable introspection, but if you are open to the reality that these quetions point towards, you may realize something very important about yourself and the world around you.

1. I can be sure that if I need legal or medical help, my race will not work against me.

2. I do not have to educate my children to be aware of systemic racism for their own daily physical protection.

3. When interacting with law enforcement, I can be sure I will not be suspected as a criminal because of my race.

4. I can sign up for counseling and expect my therapist to be the same race as me.

5. I can go shopping, well assured that I will not be followed or harassed.

6. I can turn on the television or computer and see people of my race widely represented.

7. When I am told about our american national heritage, I am shown that people of my color made it what it is.

8. There is no history of laws written against people of my race in the country in which I live.

9. I can be pretty sure of having my voice heard in a group in which I am the only member of my race.

10. My ideas of race and whiteness are seen as intelletual and non-confrontational.
